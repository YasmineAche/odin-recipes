Algerian Ghribia

Description

Algerian ghribiya is a traditional shortbread cookie made with flour, sugar, and oil or butter, known for its crumbly, melt-in-the-mouth texture.

It’s often served during holidays and celebrations, especially alongside tea or coffee.

Ingredients

  • 250 ml oil
  • 120 g icing sugar
  • 500 g self-raising flour
  • Pinch of salt
  • Ground cinnamon

Steps

  1. In a medium sized mixing bowl pour in the oil and sugar. Mix with a spoon till there are no lumps.
  2. Add the salt.
  3. Gradually add the flour mixing till incorporated.
  4. You can knead with your hands till a soft crumbly dough has formed.
  5. Spoon 1 tablespoon of the biscuit mixture onto your hand and roll till you form a small ball.
  6. Place on prepared baking tray. Using the end of a spoon, poke a small hole on top of each biscuit. Sprinkle a little cinnamon in each hole.
  7. Bake at 140 degrees for 25 – 30 minutes until slightly golden on top.
  8. Allow to cool. Enjoy!
